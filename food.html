Nice walk with nice foods!

  1. Walk on Dizengoff Street. It has some nice stores. on the street, go to Dizengoff Square. new renovated square. Great Coffee - Kafa Nahat (on Dizengoff Square) https://goo.gl/maps/fQpj5r28gNCnYVfn6
  2. Dizengoff Center is a famous mall in TLV. known for its architecture inside that makes you get lost inside. not mandatory but nice to know.
  3. then you can walk on King George street.
  4. for great street food (Falafel, Sabich, Shawarma) - go to "HaKosem" (the magician in hebrew) https://goo.gl/maps/MfkGoQ2kpQa9YAwf7
  5. on the other direction - a cool place that serves good stuff in pita in a Mediterranean vibe (good choice for vegeterian) -Miznon at King George Steert. try their Cauliflower, or something in a pita (ask the cashier) https://goo.gl/maps/JKhh6LrBJ9UHhE1YA
  6. From King George you can walk to Habima Platz https://goo.gl/maps/p3tDm24VR9HqKDkh6
  7. and then, walk down Rothschild Boulevard. it's a nicest street in tel aviv. a lot of resturants, people. cool vibe. the cool resturants/pub are in the south part of the street. You can then go to Neve Tzedek, an old neighborhood that have now a really cool vibe.
  8. Alternantive, from King George you can go to Carmel Market, and Nachalat Binaymin artists market. sit at Hamalabiya and eat Malabi, and nice desert (10nis!).
  9. Levinsky Market!

Good places for cheap food

Sabich

Sabih is an Israeli sandwich based on a traditional Iraqi Jewish dish prepared for Shabbat.
It consists of pita stuffed with fried eggplant, hard boiled eggs, Israeli salad, parsley, amba and tahini sauce.[1]

  1. Sabich Tchernichovsky- probably the best sabih in tel aviv. ask for the one with the cheese. a dish costs 23 NIS.
  2. Sabih Frishman

Falafel / Shawarma

  1. Hakosem - probably the best Falafel/Shawarma in tel aviv. Everything there is good.
  2. Mifgash Osher ("Happiness meeting place") - falafel with Labane, which is some kinf of cheese. Nice concept.
  3. Yashka - chains of falafel/shawarma.
  4. Falafel Ratzon - 6 NIS falafel!

More food in a Pita (including Meat!)

  1. Miznon - a small chain of resturants by chef Eyal Shani which is obsessed with tomatoes. They have unique food in a pita - just ask the cashier (since their menu is really empty) what is recommended. The cauliflower is a very famous dish.
  2. Jasmino - the most famous and hyped place for kebab and meat on a pita. ask the cashier what is recommended. not that cheap.
  3. Shmuel - another good kebab on the Carmel Market. They also have "mixed meats" ["Me-o-rav"] in a pita which are great.

Hummus

there are a lot of places. If you go to Akko, try "Humus Sa-id".

  1. Abu Hassan - probably the best Humus in tel aviv. ask for "Ma-sa-ba-ha" which is a Hummus mixed with tahini and "gargirim". Enjoy!

Sweets

We have a nice sweets in israel. Malabi, Knafeh & Baklava (Bak-la-waa).

  1. Hamalabiya - Malabi is a 10 NIS dessert - milk pudding with rose water on top, and nuts/pisatico on top. We love it!!!! they opened a new branch on Ibn Gebirol st. but the branch next to Carmel Market has a great vibe.
  2. Knafeh & Baklava (Bak-la-waa) - Try in Jaffa or in Carmel Matket. generaly everywhere it would be great. A new place which suppose to be really good is yaffa knafeh, but they take break between 14-15 and not open on sundays.
  3. La Gaterie

Coffe
